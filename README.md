📘 AI-Based Library of Ideological and Political Education Elements in University Courses

📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on the development and evaluation of an Artificial Intelligence-Based Ideological and Political Education (IPE) Library designed for university courses. The system aims to enhance teaching quality, improve curriculum integration, and support the accurate identification of ideological and political education elements within academic content.

The study examines how AI-driven educational tools influence teaching innovation, student engagement, and the effectiveness of the teaching–learning process.

🎯 Research Objectives
RO1: Examine the effect of the AI-Based IPE Library on the accuracy of IPE element identification in university courses.
RO2: Evaluate its impact on curriculum resource integration efficiency and teaching innovation.
RO3: Analyze its influence on student learning engagement and perceived quality of IPE.
RO4: Investigate the mediating role of teaching–learning process effectiveness in the relationship between AI-based tools and IPE identification accuracy.

❓ Research Questions
RQ1: How does the AI-Based IPE Library influence the accuracy of IPE element identification?
RQ2: How does it affect curriculum integration efficiency and teaching innovation?
RQ3: What is its impact on student engagement and perceived IPE quality?
RQ4: Does teaching–learning effectiveness mediate the relationship between AI tools and IPE identification accuracy?

🧪 Hypotheses
H1: AI-based IPE library positively affects identification accuracy.
H2: It improves curriculum resource integration efficiency.
H3: It enhances teaching innovation in university courses.
H4: It increases student learning engagement.
H5: It improves perceived quality of ideological and political education.
H6: Teaching–learning process effectiveness mediates the relationship between AI-based library use and identification accuracy.

👥 Methodology

📊 Research Design
Quantitative research approach
Mediation analysis using Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)

👨‍🎓 Sample
Target population: University students
Sample size: 574 participants
Sampling method: Random sampling

📋 Data Collection
Structured questionnaire
5-point Likert scale (1 = Strongly Disagree to 5 = Strongly Agree)

📈 Data Analysis Techniques
Descriptive statistics (Mean, SD, Frequency)
Reliability analysis (Cronbach’s Alpha > 0.70)
Validity testing (CR, AVE)
Correlation analysis
Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)
Mediation analysis (t-values, p-values, beta coefficients)

📊 Constructs Measured
AI-Based IPE Library
IPE Identification Accuracy
Curriculum Resource Integration Efficiency
Teaching Innovation
Student Learning Engagement
Perceived Quality of IPE
Teaching–Learning Process Effectiveness
